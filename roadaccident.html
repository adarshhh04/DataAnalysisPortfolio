Project Details

Project Goal: Road Accident Analysis for Enhanced Safety and Prevention

Road accidents pose a serious risk to public safety, leading to loss of life, injuries, and economic consequences. This project aims to analyze road accident data to identify key factors contributing to accidents, such as weather conditions, road types, junction control, time of occurrence, and vehicle involvement. By uncovering trends and high-risk areas, the study will provide valuable insights for traffic authorities, urban planners, and policymakers to implement targeted safety measures, improve road infrastructure, and reduce accident rates effectively.

Setup

This project analyzes road accident data to identify key risk factors and improve safety measures. The setup includes:
1.Data Collection – Gathering accident records with details on location, time, severity, weather, and road conditions.
2.Data Preprocessing – Cleaning and organizing data by handling missing values, standardizing formats, and removing duplicates.
3.Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) – Identifying accident trends, hotspots, and correlations between factors like weather and severity.
4.Data Visualization – Creating dashboards with heatmaps, severity distribution, and accident frequency insights.
5.Insights & Recommendations – Suggesting safety improvements for traffic management, road infrastructure, and policy enhancements.

Fig 1. This image shows the dataset after it was cleaned.

These steps were essential to ensure that the data was properly formatted for analysis and would yield accurate results. After cleaning now we have 307973 records.

Analysis

Now that we have clean data, we can perform exploratory data analysis to gain insights and patterns in the data.

Business Questions:

  1. What are the most accident-prone locations, and what factors contribute to high accident rates in these areas?
  2. How do weather and road conditions impact the severity and frequency of accidents?
  3. What time of day and days of the week see the highest number of accidents?
  4. Which types of vehicles are most frequently involved in accidents, and how does vehicle type influence accident severity?
  5. What role do speed limits and road infrastructure play in accident occurrences?
  6. Are certain age groups or demographics more vulnerable to accidents based on historical data?
  7. How can authorities use accident patterns to implement better traffic regulations and preventive measures?
  8. What actionable recommendations can be made to reduce accident rates and enhance road safety?

By answering these questions, the project provides valuable insights to policymakers, traffic authorities, and urban planners for developing data-driven safety strategies. 🚦

I analyzed road accident data by creating pivot tables to categorize accidents based on factors such as location, severity, time, weather conditions, and vehicle type. Using these grouped insights, I developed visualizations, including charts and dashboards, to highlight key trends. Additionally, various functions were applied to perform calculations, enabling a deeper understanding of accident patterns and risk factors.

Fig 2. This image shows the different pivot tables.

To make accident data analysis more accessible and insightful, I created a dashboard that visually presents key trends and patterns. The dashboard includes interactive charts displaying accident frequency, severity distribution, and high-risk locations. Additionally, I integrated slicers, enabling users to filter data based on factors such as weather conditions, time of day, and accident severity. This interactive approach makes it easier to identify critical risk factors and accident-prone areas, helping authorities make data-driven safety improvements. 🚦

Key Findings

Using the dashboard, we were able to determine the following insights:

  • Accident Hotspots Identified – Certain locations experience a significantly higher number of accidents, indicating the need for targeted safety measures.
  • Weather Conditions Impact Accidents – Rainy and foggy conditions contribute to increased accident severity due to reduced visibility and slippery roads.
  • Time of Day Matters – Most accidents occur during peak traffic hours, particularly in the evening, highlighting the need for better traffic management.
  • Speeding is a Major Factor – A strong correlation exists between higher speed limits and accident severity, emphasizing the need for stricter enforcement.
  • Junctions are High-Risk Zones – A significant proportion of accidents occur at intersections, often due to poor signaling or lack of traffic control.
  • Two-Wheelers and Heavy Vehicles Involved Frequently – Motorcycles and heavy trucks are more prone to accidents, suggesting a need for enhanced safety regulations for these vehicle types.
  • Lighting Conditions Affect Accident Rates – Poorly lit roads see a higher number of accidents at night, indicating the importance of improved street lighting.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis of road accident data, the following recommendations can help improve road safety and reduce accident occurrences:

  • Enhance Road Infrastructure – Improve lighting, signage, and road quality in accident-prone areas to reduce risks.
  • Implement Stricter Speed Regulations – Enforce speed limits, especially in high-risk zones, to minimize severe accidents.
  • Improve Traffic Management – Optimize signal timing, introduce better lane management, and install smart traffic monitoring systems.
  • Weather-Specific Safety Measures – Deploy warning systems and adaptive speed limits during adverse weather conditions to prevent accidents.
  • Increase Public Awareness – Conduct safety campaigns and driver education programs to promote responsible driving behavior.

This project identifies key factors contributing to road accidents, including high-risk locations, weather conditions, and speeding. Insights from the analysis can help authorities implement targeted safety measures, such as better infrastructure, stricter enforcement, and public awareness campaigns. By leveraging data-driven strategies, this study supports efforts to reduce accidents and improve road safety. 🚦
